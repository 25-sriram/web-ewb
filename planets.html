The Wildest Planets

Strangest Worlds in the Milky Way

1. HD 189733B – Rains Glass Sideways

Winds over 5,000 mph hurl glass shards sideways, making it one of the most dangerous atmospheres ever detected.

2. 55 Cancri e – Possibly Made of Diamonds

This carbon-rich planet might literally be a giant diamond under immense pressure—true cosmic luxury!

3. WASP-12b – Absorbs Almost All Light

Darker than asphalt, this planet swallows nearly all light, making it act like a mini-black hole planet.

4. Kepler-16b – Orbits Two Suns

This planet orbits two stars, giving it double sunrises and sunsets—straight out of Star Wars.

5. PSR B1620-26 b – 12.7 Billion Years Old

Older than Earth itself, this ancient planet formed when the universe was still a baby—pure alien vibes!

6. HD 106906 b – Orbits Crazy Far Out

This planet orbits so far from its star it’s barely bound—almost like a rogue planet doing its own thing.

7. WASP-76b – Rains Molten Iron

Extreme heat vaporizes iron, which later condenses and rains down as molten metal. Wild and fiery!

8. Gliese 436b – Has Burning Ice

Water stays frozen solid even at scorching temperatures—gravity creates burning ice.
